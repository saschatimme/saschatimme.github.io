I am a second year PhD student at TU Berlin under the supervision of Michael Joswig and Bernd Sturmfels. My main field of interest is Numerical Nonlinear Algebra, a subject at the intersection of Numerical Analysis and Algebraic Geometry. Together with Paul Breiding I am developing the Julia package HomotopyContinuation.jl, a package to solve systems of polynomial equations by numerical homotopy continuation methods.

I am supported by the Research Training Group Facets of Complexity and the Einstein Fellowship research group Nonlinear Algebra of Bernd Sturmfels.

Prior to my PhD I was a software engineer at the start-up Math 42 which was successfully aquired by Chegg in October 2017.

Interests. Nonlinear Algebra, Polynomial System Solving, Julia, Numerical Computations

Software Projects

  • HomotopyContinuation.jl: A Julia package for the numerical solution of polynomial systems by homotopy continuation methods.
  • Polymake.jl: A Julia interface to polymake.
  • MixedSubdivisions.jl: A Julia package for computing a (fine) mixed subdivision and the mixed volume of lattice polytopes.
  • PolynomialAmoebas.jl: Visualization and computations related to Amoebas in 2 and 3 variables.
